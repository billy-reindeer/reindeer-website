Hoveringham, Nottinghamshire

Ten minutes from Southwell, in the Trent Valley

The Reindeer is in Hoveringham, a village just south of Southwell on the edge of the Trent Valley. From the Minster town, it is a short drive through Thurgarton and along the river, the kind of Sunday morning journey that sets the day up right.

The terrace at the back looks directly onto the village cricket ground. On a good Sunday, there are few better places to spend a couple of hours in Nottinghamshire.

Billy Mantle · Chef and Owner

A kitchen that takes produce seriously

The Reindeer is run by Billy Mantle, a professional chef with serious restaurant experience. The Sunday roast here is not an afterthought added to prop up a quiet service. It is the meal the pub was built around.

The Reindeer exists for one reason: to serve the very best produce we can get our hands on. Great ingredients have a story behind them: the people who raise them, grow them and care for them. Our responsibility is to honour that work by cooking with skill, restraint and attention to detail.

Simple in principle. Uncompromising in execution.

Billy Mantle

Every Sunday · 12 to 6

The Sunday roast, done properly

Choose from dry-aged beef, roast pork belly, corn-fed chicken or wild mushroom pithivier, all served with roast potatoes, seasonal vegetables, Yorkshire pudding and gravy.

The beef is dry-aged by Dunwood Farms in Staffordshire. The chicken comes from Packington Farms. The producers are named on the menu because they earn it.

3 courses £39

Questions about Sunday lunch near Southwell

The Reindeer Hoveringham is 10 minutes from Southwell via the B6386 through Thurgarton. Sunday lunch is served every week from 12 to 6pm on a terrace overlooking the village cricket ground. Free parking on site.

From Southwell, head south on the B6386 towards Thurgarton, then follow signs for Hoveringham. The pub is on Main Street in the centre of the village, NG14 7JR. The drive takes around 10 minutes.

Full directions with map

Sunday lunch is £33 for 2 courses and £39 for 3 courses. All roasts are served with roast potatoes, seasonal vegetables, Yorkshire pudding and gravy.

See the full Sunday menu

Booking is strongly recommended, particularly in summer when the terrace fills quickly. Walk-ins are welcome at the bar.

Book a table online or call 07975 884 881.

Parking is available on site and roadside on Main Street. Spaces can be limited on busy Sundays, so arriving a few minutes early is recommended.
